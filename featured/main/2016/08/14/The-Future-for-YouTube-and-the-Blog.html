The Future for YouTube and the Blog

Tom

14 Aug 2016
On the topic of YouTube, if you have been subscribed to my YouTube channel, you would know that I haven’t been uploading content as frequently as I used to. I feel as if I have been unclear as to why this is due to the mass amounts of messages I’ve received on the topic of “I’m quitting YouTube”, I can say with reassurance, that this is not the case and I have no intention to completely abandon YouTube or uploading content. From when I started the channel, I attempted to upload a video every week however when I moved into my senior year of school (I am a student) the workload increased significantly and changed to one video every two weeks. Now that I’m dedicating a significant portion of time to making this blog successful, I’m going to completely remove the uploading schedule however still attempt to create frequent content where I can.


Another reason for this change of uploading frequency is the recent undertaking of the Instagram 365 photo challenge and the administration of the Simpligon Instagram page. If you were unaware the Instagram 365 photo challenge it requires you to upload a photo every day for a year and has taken up a significant amount of my time surprisingly. I’m also now going to be running the Simpligon Instagram page which is going to be an extension of the blog where I can share cool things I find online in one place. If you want to follow me on my main Instagram account and the Simpligon Instagram account be sure to click the links below.
Main Instagram
Simpligon Instagram


On the topic of Simpligon this is my newest and most ambitious project I’ve yet to create. In short Simpligon is a web blog/magazine focused on exploring the best of culture, technology and inspiration. Things that will be posted include weekly wallpapers and inspiration, posts on what’s happening in culture and technology as well as content dedicated to desk/office setups. At the moment Simpligon is in it’s very early stages and I understand that there are several visual glitches within the site and it’s very lackluster on features. In the coming weeks most of the bugs should be solved and more features like a search field and better navigation will be implemented. If you would like to submit an issue with the site or you can’t stand a design decision I made, you can contact me at simpligon@gmail.com or on Instagram as a comment.


Lastly, I would also like to note that I will no longer be using the Sony a6300 camera as I will not be focusing on YouTube as much but instead on taking minimalist photos for my personal Instagram and Simpligon. After researching, I decided to pick up the Panasonic GM1 and it will now be my main camera for Instagram and YouTube. It takes some pretty crispy photos.
